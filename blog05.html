IP rights and their importance in the creative industries

Julia Gongala

Oct 14, 2022 4 min

What is the difference between a copyright, patent, and trademark? How do they protect your intellectual property as a programmer? This article will show overview of what each one protects and why you want one.


What intellectual property (IP) is?

Intellectual property or IP refers to things that come form the mid. Inventions, designs, artistic and literary works, thing you make or produce, and the names of products and brands. Intellectual property belongs to a person who though it up or put the work into creating it. They get to decide who makes it, how and where it is used, and even who can sell and profit from it. You might think if intellectual property comes from the mind, how it would be possible to steal it? After all it is not like you can break into an office and steal logo, can you? Not quite. Logos can be copied, designs can be tweaked, names can be nicked. However, IP is protected in law. This means that people who come up with these ideas can be recognised and paid for their work, inventions, or creations.


How to protect your intellectual property



How can thinkers and creators keep their IP safe from misuse by others after all in a digital world is easy to copy an idea or design. Luckily there are laws in place to protect IP. Once the person is ready to go public with it there are three major paths to legal protection.



Applying for a patent

Patens are designed to cover things like inventions, new processes, new machines, and new ways of manufacturing. In United Kingdom you can register your patent here


Applying for copyrights

Copyrights are designed to protect works like art, music, writings, movies, and software. In United Kingdom you can register your copyright here


Applying for a trademark

Trademarks are designed to protect unique branding and identifiers like business names, logos, slogans or even mascots. In United Kingdom you can register your trademark here


Trade secret

Keeping something secret trade makes sure that things like manufacturing processes, formulas, or compilations of information never make it into the wrong hands.
